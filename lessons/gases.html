Topic 6

Gases

Gases are the simplest state of matter to model mathematically. The ideal gas law connects pressure, volume, temperature, and moles into one powerful equation. Understanding when gases deviate from ideal behavior is equally important.

📐 The Ideal Gas Law

PV = nRT P = pressure (atm) V = volume (L) n = moles T = temperature (K — always Kelvin!) R = 0.08206 L·atm/mol·K
⚠️ Always convert temperature to Kelvin: K = °C + 273.15. Using Celsius is one of the most common AP mistakes.
Example: What pressure does 2.00 mol of gas exert in a 5.00 L container at 300 K?
P = nRT/V = (2.00 × 0.08206 × 300) / 5.00 = 9.85 atm

🔄 The Gas Laws

LawVariablesEquation
Boyle's LawP and V (constant n, T)P₁V₁ = P₂V₂
Charles's LawV and T (constant n, P)V₁/T₁ = V₂/T₂
Gay-Lussac's LawP and T (constant n, V)P₁/T₁ = P₂/T₂
Avogadro's LawV and n (constant P, T)V₁/n₁ = V₂/n₂
Combined Gas LawP, V, TP₁V₁/T₁ = P₂V₂/T₂
STP: Standard Temperature and Pressure = 0°C (273.15 K) and 1 atm. At STP, 1 mole of ideal gas occupies 22.4 L.
