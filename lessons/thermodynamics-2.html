Topic 11

Thermodynamics II — Entropy & Gibbs Free Energy

This unit connects enthalpy and entropy into one powerful concept — Gibbs free energy — which predicts whether a reaction will spontaneously occur. It also ties thermodynamics directly to equilibrium.

🌀 Entropy (S)

Entropy is a measure of disorder or randomness in a system. Nature tends toward higher entropy — this is the Second Law of Thermodynamics.

Entropy increases when:

  • Solids melt → liquids, or liquids vaporize → gases
  • A substance dissolves into solution
  • Moles of gas increase during a reaction
  • Temperature increases
  • More complex molecules form from simpler ones
ΔS°rxn = Σ S°(products) − Σ S°(reactants) Units: J/mol·K (note: joules, not kilojoules!)
AP Tip: The Third Law states that a perfect crystal at 0 K has S = 0. This means absolute entropy values (S°) are always positive. ΔS can be negative or positive.

⚡ Gibbs Free Energy (G)

Gibbs free energy combines enthalpy and entropy into one criterion for spontaneity. A reaction is spontaneous when ΔG < 0.

ΔG = ΔH − TΔS ΔG° = ΔH° − TΔS° (standard conditions) ΔG° = Σ ΔG°f(products) − Σ ΔG°f(reactants) T must be in Kelvin
ΔHΔSΔGSpontaneous?
− (exothermic)+ (more disorder)Always −Always spontaneous
+ (endothermic)− (less disorder)Always +Never spontaneous

Example: A reaction has ΔH° = −92 kJ/mol and ΔS° = −198 J/mol·K. At what temperature does spontaneity switch?
Set ΔG = 0: 0 = ΔH − TΔS → T = ΔH/ΔS
T = −92,000 ÷ −198 = 465 K
Below 465 K: spontaneous (ΔG < 0). Above 465 K: nonspontaneous.

🔗 ΔG° and Equilibrium

Standard free energy and the equilibrium constant are directly related. This is one of the most important connections in all of AP Chemistry.

ΔG° = −RT ln K R = 8.314 J/mol·K T in Kelvin K = equilibrium constant Also: ΔG = ΔG° + RT ln Q (tells you spontaneity at non-standard conditions)
ΔG°KMeaning
NegativeK > 1Products favored at equilibrium
ZeroK = 1Roughly equal products and reactants
PositiveK < 1Reactants favored at equilibrium
Example: Calculate K at 298 K for a reaction with ΔG° = −17.1 kJ/mol.
−17,100 = −(8.314)(298) ln K
ln K = 17,100 ÷ 2477.6 = 6.90
K = e^6.90 = 992 (products strongly favored)

🔋 ΔG° and Electrochemistry Connection

ΔG° = −nFE°cell n = moles of electrons transferred F = Faraday's constant = 96,485 C/mol E°cell = standard cell potential (volts)
Key relationships to memorize: Spontaneous reaction → ΔG° < 0 → K > 1 → E°cell > 0. These three criteria all say the same thing — products are favored.
