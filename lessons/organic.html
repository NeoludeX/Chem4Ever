Topic 14

Organic Chemistry

Organic chemistry is the chemistry of carbon-containing compounds. Carbon's ability to form four bonds and chain together makes an enormous variety of molecules possible. The AP exam focuses on functional groups, reaction types, and structure-property relationships.

🔗 Hydrocarbons

Hydrocarbons contain only carbon and hydrogen. They are the backbone of organic chemistry.

🔄 Isomers

Isomers are compounds with the same molecular formula but different structural arrangements.

  • Constitutional (structural) isomers: atoms connected differently (e.g., butane vs isobutane, both C₄H₁₀)
  • Stereoisomers: same connectivity, different spatial arrangement
    • cis/trans isomers: occur in alkenes due to restricted rotation around C=C; cis = same side, trans = opposite sides
    • Enantiomers: mirror images that cannot be superimposed (chiral molecules)

🧬 Polymers

Polymers are large molecules made from repeating monomer units. Two main types:

  • Addition polymers: monomers with double bonds add together with no byproduct. Example: polyethylene from ethylene (CH₂=CH₂)ₙ
  • Condensation polymers: monomers join with loss of a small molecule (usually H₂O). Examples: nylon (polyamide), polyester, proteins, DNA
