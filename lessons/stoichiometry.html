Topic 4

Stoichiometry

Stoichiometry is the math of chemistry — using balanced equations and mole ratios to calculate how much of each substance reacts or is produced. Every quantitative AP problem traces back to these skills.

⚖️ Balancing Chemical Equations

Atoms are conserved in chemical reactions — you can't create or destroy them. A balanced equation has equal numbers of each atom on both sides. Only adjust coefficients (never subscripts).

Example: Combustion of propane Unbalanced: C₃H₈ + O₂ → CO₂ + H₂O
Balanced: C₃H₈ + 5O₂ → 3CO₂ + 4H₂O
Check: C(3=3), H(8=8), O(10=10) ✓

🔢 Mole Ratios & Stoichiometry

Coefficients in a balanced equation are mole ratios. They let you convert between moles of any two substances in the reaction.

moles of A → (mole ratio) → moles of B → (× molar mass) → grams of B
Example: How many grams of CO₂ are produced from 44 g of C₃H₈?
Molar mass C₃H₈ = 44.09 g/mol → 44 ÷ 44.09 = 0.998 mol C₃H₈
Mole ratio: 1 mol C₃H₈ → 3 mol CO₂ → 0.998 × 3 = 2.994 mol CO₂
Mass = 2.994 × 44.01 = 131.8 g CO₂

🚦 Limiting Reagent & Percent Yield

The limiting reagent is the reactant that runs out first and determines the maximum amount of product. The other reactant(s) are in excess.

How to find it: Convert each reactant to moles of product using its mole ratio. Whichever gives the least product is the limiting reagent.

Percent Yield = (actual yield ÷ theoretical yield) × 100%
Example: 10.0 g N₂ reacts with 3.0 g H₂ to form NH₃ (N₂ + 3H₂ → 2NH₃). What is the limiting reagent?
N₂: 10.0 ÷ 28.02 = 0.357 mol → 0.357 × 2 = 0.714 mol NH₃
H₂: 3.0 ÷ 2.016 = 1.488 mol → 1.488 ÷ 3 = 0.496 mol NH₃
H₂ gives less NH₃ → H₂ is the limiting reagent. Max yield = 0.496 mol NH₃ = 8.44 g

🧪 Empirical & Molecular Formulas

The empirical formula is the simplest whole-number ratio of atoms. The molecular formula is the actual number of atoms (a whole-number multiple of the empirical formula).

Step 1: Assume 100 g sample → % directly = grams Step 2: Convert grams to moles (÷ molar mass) Step 3: Divide all by the smallest mole value Step 4: Multiply to get whole numbers if needed Step 5: Molecular formula = empirical formula × n, where n = molar mass ÷ empirical formula mass
Example: A compound is 40.0% C, 6.7% H, 53.3% O. Molar mass = 60 g/mol. Find the molecular formula.
C: 40.0÷12.01 = 3.33 mol; H: 6.7÷1.008 = 6.65 mol; O: 53.3÷16.00 = 3.33 mol
Divide by 3.33: C=1, H=2, O=1 → Empirical formula: CH₂O (mass = 30 g/mol)
n = 60 ÷ 30 = 2 → Molecular formula: C₂H₄O₂

💧 Solution Stoichiometry

Molarity (M) = moles of solute ÷ liters of solution moles = M × V (in liters) Dilution: M₁V₁ = M₂V₂
Example: What volume of 0.500 M HCl is needed to react with 25.0 mL of 0.300 M NaOH?
HCl + NaOH → NaCl + H₂O (1:1 ratio)
moles NaOH = 0.0250 × 0.300 = 0.00750 mol
Volume HCl = 0.00750 ÷ 0.500 = 0.0150 L = 15.0 mL
