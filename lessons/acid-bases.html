Topic 10

Acids & Bases

Acid-base chemistry is one of the most heavily tested units on the AP exam. You need to be able to calculate pH, work with weak acid/base equilibria, design buffers, and interpret titration curves.

🧪 Definitions

DefinitionAcidBase
ArrheniusProduces H⁺ in waterProduces OH⁻ in water
Brønsted-LowryProton (H⁺) donorProton (H⁺) acceptor
LewisElectron pair acceptorElectron pair donor

The Brønsted-Lowry definition is most used on the AP exam. Every acid-base reaction involves a conjugate acid-base pair — when an acid donates H⁺, it becomes its conjugate base.

📊 pH Scale & Water Equilibrium

Kw = [H⁺][OH⁻] = 1.0×10⁻¹⁴ at 25°C pH = −log[H⁺] pOH = −log[OH⁻] pH + pOH = 14 [H⁺] = 10^(−pH)

💪 Strong vs Weak Acids & Bases

Strong acids/bases dissociate 100% in water. Memorize these:

  • Strong acids: HCl, HBr, HI, HNO₃, H₂SO₄, HClO₄
  • Strong bases: Group 1 hydroxides (NaOH, KOH, etc.), Ca
