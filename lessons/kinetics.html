Topic 8

Kinetics

Kinetics answers the question: how fast does a reaction occur, and why? Thermodynamics tells you if a reaction is favorable — kinetics tells you whether it'll happen in a millisecond or a million years.

📈 Reaction Rates

Reaction rate is the change in concentration of a reactant or product per unit time. Rate is always expressed as a positive number.

For aA + bB → cC + dD: Rate = −(1/a)Δ[A]/Δt = −(1/b)Δ[B]/Δt = (1/c)Δ[C]/Δt = (1/d)Δ[D]/Δt

Factors that increase reaction rate:

  • Increase concentration — more collisions per second
  • Increase temperature — faster molecules, more collisions exceed activation energy
  • Increase surface area — more exposed particles available to react
  • Add a catalyst — lowers activation energy without being consumed

📋 Rate Laws & Reaction Order

The rate law is determined experimentally — you cannot read it from the balanced equation (unless it's an elementary step).

Rate = k[A]^m [B]^n k = rate constant m = order in A n = order in B Overall order = m + n

To find orders from experimental data, compare trials where one concentration changes and the other stays constant:

Example: Find the rate law from this data for A + B → products:
Trial 1: [A]=0.10, [B]=0.10, Rate=2.0×10⁻³
Trial 2: [A]=0.20, [B]=0.10, Rate=4.0×10⁻³
Trial 3: [A]=0.10, [B]=0.20, Rate=2.0×10⁻³

Trials 1→2: [A] doubles, rate doubles → 1st order in A (m=1)
Trials 1→3: [B] doubles, rate unchanged → 0th order in B (n=0)
Rate = k[A]¹ → k = Rate/[A] = 2.0×10⁻³/0.10 = 0.020 M⁻¹s⁻¹... wait — 0.020 s⁻¹

🌡️ Activation Energy & the Arrhenius Equation

Activation energy (Ea) is the minimum energy colliding molecules need for a reaction to occur. A catalyst provides an alternative pathway with lower Ea — it speeds the reaction without changing ΔH.

Arrhenius Equation: k = Ae^(−Ea/RT) ln(k₂/k₁) = (Ea/R)(1/T₁ − 1/T₂) R = 8.314 J/mol·K Ea in J/mol T in Kelvin
Example: A reaction has k = 0.0110 s⁻¹ at 300 K and k = 0.0870 s⁻¹ at 350 K. Find Ea.
ln(0.0870/0.0110) = (Ea/8.314)(1/300 − 1/350)
ln(7.909) = (Ea/8.314)(4.762×10⁻⁴)
2.068 = Ea × 5.727×10⁻⁵
Ea = 36,110 J/mol ≈ 36.1 kJ/mol

🔬 Reaction Mechanisms

Most reactions occur in multiple steps called elementary steps. The sequence of steps is the reaction mechanism. The slowest step is the rate-determining step (RDS) — it controls the overall rate.

  • For elementary steps only: you CAN write the rate law directly from the equation
  • Unimolecular step: Rate = k[A]
  • Bimolecular step: Rate = k[A][B]
  • A reaction intermediate is produced in one step and consumed in another — it does not appear in the overall equation or the rate law
AP Tip: If a proposed mechanism's slow step rate law matches the experimental rate law, the mechanism is consistent with experiment. You cannot prove a mechanism — only rule it out.
